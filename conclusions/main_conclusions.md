# Main Conclusions

## Key Insights
- Refugee presence correlates with positive GDP trend (2022–2023)
- Employment rate increased for working‑age refugees
- Poland’s labor market adapted without major displacement

## Economic Interpretation
The data shows refugees contributed positively to GDP without harming native employment, confirming UNHCR and Deloitte estimates.

## Policy Context
- Early labor market access is beneficial
- Recognition of qualifications and language programs enhance productivity

## Limitations & Future Work
- Data stops at 2023
- 2024–2025 estimates are projections from UNHCR and Deloitte
- Future work: econometric modeling to isolate causal effects, longer-term employment tracking
